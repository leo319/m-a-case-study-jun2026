# Card 03 — Deal mechanism & synergies

**Bucket:** Deal / combination
**The why it answers:** Given the industry (01) and the two companies (02), what concretely
changes for the acquirer when these combine — through what operating lever — and is that
worth the price?
**Grounded in:** Value-chain overlap (Porter); Damodaran, *The Value of Synergy*
(operating vs. financial synergy); the empirical split that cost synergies are far more
realizable than revenue synergies.

## First-principles trace (do this FIRST)
- How is the deal structured (consideration, financing, conditions, timeline, ownership)?
- **What does the combined entity do that neither side could alone, and through what
  operating lever?** (Overlapping routes → density on fixed assets; shared plants →
  utilization; cross-sell into an installed base.) Name the lever, then trace it to a cost or
  revenue line.
- Does the combination *change the unit economics* (card 00/01), or just make the acquirer
  bigger? Bigger is not better unless the lever moves a cost or price.

## Framework prompts (test the trace — adapt or discard)
- **Deal type follows from the mechanism**, not the other way: horizontal / capability /
  geographic is a *consequence* of what the lever is. Explain the operating lever first, then
  name the deal type as a consequence of it.
- **Damodaran synergy split:** classify each claimed synergy as **cost** or **revenue** (or
  financial). **Discount revenue synergies by default** — studies put cost synergies at
  ~70–85% of realized value vs. ~25–35% for revenue. Credit a revenue synergy only with a
  named mechanism.
- **Break-even test:** what synergy level is needed to offset the premium / financing
  dilution? Modest synergies needed → lower-risk deal; massive synergies needed → success
  hinges on execution.

## Facts to gather (→ research)
- Premium vs. the **unaffected** pre-announcement price; consideration & financing mix;
  pro-forma leverage; accretion/dilution disclosure.
- Every disclosed valuation anchor (each advisor's comparables, precedent transactions, DCF,
  premiums-paid range) — captured as separate claims, no cherry-picking.
- Stated synergies ($ and timing) and management's stated basis for them.
- Operating overlap facts (route/plant/footprint overlap, shared customers) that a synergy
  lever would run through.

## Inferences to form (→ expert)
- The synergy **mechanism** — the causal chain from a specific cost-structure fact to the cost or
  revenue line it moves, roughly how much, and what that says about whether the premium is
  justified. A grounded causal chain, not a verdict.
- Synergies **sized** vs. target revenue/EBITDA and vs. precedent-deal synergy %; the
  ex-synergy multiple if the headline is synergy-inclusive.
- The few deal-economics risks that actually threaten the lever (people retention, route/
  sales-force overlap, customer attrition, integration), each with its transmission to returns.

## Weak-answer tells
- "Creates a leading platform" / a bare "horizontal" label with no lever traced to a number.
- Revenue synergies credited at face value with no mechanism.
- Premium stated but never connected to *why this buyer* will pay it.
- One advisor's DCF passed off as "the" range.
