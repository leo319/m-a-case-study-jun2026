# Card 04 — Macro & exogenous forces

**Bucket:** Macro
**The why it answers:** What forces *outside* the two companies — the economy, geopolitics,
politics, regulation — bear on whether this deal happens and how it performs?
**Grounded in:** PESTEL-style scan (Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Environmental,
Legal), filtered to what is **material to this deal**.

## First-principles trace (do this FIRST)
- What in the **macro economy** does this business ride on (consumer demand, employment,
  industrial activity, rates)? Which way is it moving?
- What **regulatory / political** regime governs the deal and the industry — and is it
  tightening or loosening? (Antitrust posture especially.)
- Any **geopolitical / trade** exposure (tariffs, FX, supply-chain origin, sanctions)?
- Which of these is a *transmission channel into this deal*, not just a general risk?

## Framework prompts (test the trace — adapt or discard)
- Run the PESTEL letters as a checklist; **keep only the factors with a transmission
  mechanism to this deal** and discard the generic ones. A macro factor with no channel to
  this deal is noise.
- For antitrust, ask for the **base rate**: how have comparable sector deals cleared, been
  challenged, or required divestitures? (Not just this deal's HSR timeline.)

## Facts to gather (→ research)
- Macro exposure disclosed in risk factors (rates, FX, tariffs, demand cyclicality).
- The regulatory approvals the deal requires and the current antitrust posture.
- Precedent antitrust outcomes for comparable deals (cleared / second request / divestiture /
  blocked).
- Any geopolitical or political development specifically touching either company or the deal.

## Inferences to form (→ expert)
- Which macro/regulatory force is **material to this deal** and through what channel.
- The antitrust base-rate read: how likely is clearance, on what evidence, and what would
  change it.

## Weak-answer tells
- A generic macro list ("inflation, rates, geopolitics") with no link to this deal.
- Antitrust assessed only from this deal's timeline, with no precedent base rate.
- Treating a tough regulatory environment as a footnote when it should be a ranked risk
  (card 05's anomaly drill).
