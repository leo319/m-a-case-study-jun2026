# Card 02 — Company position & competitive advantage

**Bucket:** Companies
**The why it answers:** For *each* of acquirer and target — what do they do, where do they
sit versus the market, and what (if anything) is their durable advantage?
**Grounded in:** VRIO / Resource-Based View (Barney) — advantage comes from resources that
are Valuable, Rare, costly to Imitate, and Organized to exploit.

## First-principles trace (do this FIRST, for BOTH companies)
- What does the company actually sell, to whom, and how is revenue split **by segment
  (`$` and `%`)**?
- Where does it sit relative to the market — share, scale, growth versus peers?
- **Why do its customers stay** (or leave)? What is the real source of any advantage —
  scale, density, brand, switching costs, a cost position — or is there none?
- Who are its main competitors, and how does it win or lose against them?

## Framework prompts (test the trace — adapt or discard)
- **VRIO** on the claimed advantage: is the resource Valuable, Rare, hard to Imitate, and is
  the firm Organized to capture it? If it fails any test, the "advantage" is temporary or
  cosmetic — say so.
- Compare the two companies head-to-head on the value chain (card 01): where is each strong
  or weak, and do they overlap or complement? (Feeds card 03.)

## Facts to gather (→ research)
- Segment revenue mix (`$` and `%`), segment growth, employee/customer counts, margins.
- Market share / relative scale vs. named competitors.
- Stated strategy of each company.
- Evidence on customer retention / switching costs (churn, contract length, win-loss).

## Inferences to form (→ expert)
- One-line read on each company's competitive position and whether its advantage is durable
  (VRIO verdict), not just asserted.
- Where acquirer and target are strong/weak relative to each other — the raw material for the
  combination logic in card 03.

## Weak-answer tells
- "Market leader with strong brand" with no share number and no VRIO test.
- Segment mix dropped because "the narrative is tight" — the mix *is* the company.
- Only the target analyzed; the acquirer's position left blank (acquirer strength/weakness
  drives why it's buying and how hard it pushes).
