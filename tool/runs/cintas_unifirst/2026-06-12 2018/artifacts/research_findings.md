## Verified inferences
- UniFirst's consolidated operating margin in fiscal 2025 was approximately 7.6% ($184.5M operating income / $2,432.4M revenue = 7.58%), far below Cintas's reported ~22% operating margin, framing the central margin-gap thesis of the deal.  _(builds on target_fundamentals_4, target_fundamentals_5)_
- UniFirst's fiscal 2025 capital intensity is high: capital expenditures of $154.3 million consumed roughly 52% of its $296.9 million of operating cash flow ($154.3M / $296.9M), leaving comparatively modest free cash flow generation.  _(builds on target_fundamentals_16, target_fundamentals_17)_
- The US/Canada uniform-rental industry is a three-national-player oligopoly over a fragmented local-route base: all three public participants (Cintas, UniFirst, Vestis) independently name the same short list of national rivals — Cintas, UniFirst/Alsco, Vestis — while describing their served markets as 'local in nature, fragmented and highly competitive', confirming a structure of a few national consolidators over a long regional/local tail.  _(builds on commercial_market_7, commercial_market_9, commercial_market_12, commercial_market_13)_
- Cintas dwarfs its named rivals in scale: its fiscal 2025 total revenue of ~$10.34 billion is roughly 4.3x UniFirst's ~$2.43 billion and roughly 3.8x Vestis's ~$2.7 billion (10.34 / 2.43 ≈ 4.3x; 10.34 / 2.7 ≈ 3.8x), making the target (UniFirst) and the spun-off #2 (Vestis) each less than a quarter of the acquirer's size.  _(builds on commercial_market_3, commercial_market_14)_
