## Key questions (first-principles)

_Deal-specific questions derived from the method cards beyond the static checklist. These drive research, the expert's analysis, and the red-team's gap hunt — edit/add/drop at the gate._

### Industry
- How did the 2023 Aramark→Vestis spinoff and Vestis's subsequent struggles reshape the competitive landscape, and is Cintas's 2026 move offensive (extend its lead) or defensive (consolidate #3 before a weakened rival or PE buyer can)? _(commercial_market)_
    - _why it matters:_ The 'why now' trigger; distinguishes a strategic density play from opportunistic empire-building.

### Companies
- Is UniFirst's underperformance (the Engine Capital thesis: weak TSR under family control) a fixable execution/operating gap Cintas can close, or a structural disadvantage — i.e., is the synergy case really 'UNF was under-managed'? _(target_fundamentals)_
    - _why it matters:_ If the gap is fixable by a better operator, the synergy lever is credible; if structural, the premium is harder to justify.

### Deal & synergies
- What is the true 'unaffected' price the ~$310 premium should be measured against, given a publicly-known prior approach (Jan 2025) and an active Engine Capital campaign that likely already embedded takeover/sale speculation into UNF's stock? _(deal_rationale_synergies)_
    - _why it matters:_ If the baseline is contaminated by prior bid + activist speculation, the headline premium overstates the real control premium — central to whether Cintas overpaid.
- Through what specific operating lever (route density, plant/branch utilization, SG&A, procurement) does Cintas close UniFirst's margin gap — UNF's operating margin (~7-8%) is roughly a third of Cintas's — and how much of the $375M synergy is cost vs revenue? _(deal_rationale_synergies)_
    - _why it matters:_ Cost synergies on a density lever are far more realizable than revenue synergies; the lever is the whole value case for paying up.
- Does the ~$375M cost-synergy target (run-rate within 4 years) hold up against UniFirst's own cost base — it is roughly 2x UNF's FY25 operating income (~$184M) — and what is the ex-synergy acquisition multiple? _(deal_rationale_synergies)_
    - _why it matters:_ A synergy figure that dwarfs the target's standalone profit needs a hard mechanism, or the deal only works on heroic execution.
- How does the merger agreement allocate the antitrust risk — is there a regulatory-efforts / 'hell or high water' covenant, a divestiture cap, a reverse termination fee payable if the FTC blocks it, and what is the outside / drop-dead date? _(merger_agreement)_
    - _why it matters:_ Sets who bears the Second-Request risk and how long Cintas is committed to fight — a key input to the close-likelihood force balance.
- What do the cash/stock mix and the $2.85B bridge + revolver do to Cintas's pro-forma leverage (~1.5x net debt/EBITDA cited) and EPS — is the deal accretive or dilutive once new-debt cost and the 0.7720x shares issued are weighed against synergy phasing? _(financing_balance_sheet)_
    - _why it matters:_ Tests whether the financial structure is conservative or stretched, and whether accretion depends on the synergies actually landing.

### Macro & exogenous
- Given a combined entity approaching ~half the U.S. uniform-rental market and the FTC Second Request (Jun 11, 2026) plus competitor outreach, what is the realistic remedy path — local route/branch divestitures vs. a block — and the base rate for route-based/facility-services deals? _(antitrust_regulatory)_
    - _why it matters:_ Antitrust is the dominant close-risk; the remedy path drives both deal certainty and the economics of what Cintas actually keeps.

### Why this deal, why now
- Why did the Croatti family / UniFirst board agree in Mar 2026 after unanimously rejecting Cintas's $275 cash bid ~14 months earlier — was it the Engine Capital activist campaign / proxy fight, the higher cash+stock price, or a governance/leadership change? _(management_governance)_
    - _why it matters:_ Determines whether this is a value-maximizing arm's-length outcome or a pressured one, and what that says about price adequacy and deal certainty.
