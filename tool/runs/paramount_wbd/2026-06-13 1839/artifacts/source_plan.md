## Key questions (first-principles)

_Deal-specific questions derived from the method cards beyond the static checklist. These drive research, the expert's analysis, and the red-team's gap hunt — edit/add/drop at the gate._

### Industry
- Paramount+ and HBO Max are each sub-scale against Netflix and Disney; what does combining them concretely change in streaming unit economics — content amortization spread over a larger subscriber base, reduced churn, pricing power — is there a real density lever, or just additive scale? _(commercial_market)_
    - _why it matters:_ This is the core revenue-synergy thesis; per the synergy method a revenue synergy earns credit only with a named mechanism, not 'a bigger platform'.

### Companies
- WBD was itself about to split into Warner Bros. (studios+streaming) and Discovery Global (networks); why is Paramount buying the WHOLE company including the declining linear networks rather than only the studio/streaming crown jewels — does it want the networks' cash flow to service debt, or is it stuck absorbing a melting asset to get HBO/Warner? _(target_fundamentals)_
    - _why it matters:_ Clarifies whether Paramount is paying for an asset it values or tolerating a declining business as the price of acquiring the IP it actually wants.

### Deal & synergies
- Why is Paramount paying $31.00/share all-cash — roughly a 139% premium over WBD's undisturbed price of $12.54 (Sept 10, 2025) — and how much of that premium is contested-auction dynamics (Netflix and Comcast bidding) versus a unique strategic lever only Paramount can pull? _(deal_rationale_synergies)_
    - _why it matters:_ Separates 'won a bidding war / overpaid' from 'real synergy lever that justifies the price', which determines whether the premium is recoverable or value-destroying.
- Why did the WBD board flip from the Netflix cash-and-stock deal ($27.75/share, studios+streaming only with linear spun off) to Paramount's all-cash $31 whole-company offer — was the deciding factor price, cash certainty, or that Paramount absorbed the regulatory risk? _(deal_rationale_synergies)_
    - _why it matters:_ Reveals what the board actually weighted (headline value vs. certainty vs. risk transfer) and what WBD shareholders are really receiving versus the road not taken.
- Why fund a ~$111B all-cash deal with ~$50B+ of new debt (pro-forma ~6-7x EBITDA, ~$79-87B total debt) to buy a company whose largest segment — Global Linear Networks — is in structural decline, and how does the combined entity service that debt if linear erodes faster than planned? _(financing_balance_sheet)_
    - _why it matters:_ Tests whether the pro-forma balance sheet survives a faster cord-cutting scenario; this is the central tail risk of the transaction.
- Why did Paramount agree to seller-favorable risk allocation — a $7B reverse regulatory termination fee, paying WBD's $2.8B Netflix break fee, and a $0.25/quarter ticking fee for delays past Sept 30, 2026 — and what does that signal about Paramount's confidence in clearance versus its determination to win at any cost? _(merger_agreement)_
    - _why it matters:_ Deal-protection terms reveal each side's leverage and how hard the buyer will push to close — a key input to the close-probability force balance.
- What synergies has Paramount actually disclosed ($ amount, timing, cost vs. revenue), and what annual synergy level would be needed to offset the premium and the incremental interest burden — modest or heroic? _(deal_rationale_synergies)_
    - _why it matters:_ The break-even test: a deal needing modest synergies is lower-risk; one hinging on heroic, mostly-revenue synergies is an execution gamble.

### Macro & exogenous
- Why did the DOJ clear a combination of two major film/TV studios plus CBS News and CNN with NO divestitures and no conditions on June 12, 2026, against a historically tougher media-antitrust backdrop — what explains it (streaming-era market definition, or political alignment of the Ellison ownership with the administration)? _(antitrust_regulatory)_
    - _why it matters:_ The clearance is the pivotal certainty event; understanding WHY it cleared tells us how durable that clearance is against the threatened state-AG challenge.
- What is the substance and likelihood of the threatened multi-state attorneys-general lawsuit — and the surrounding political controversy (FCC 'capitulation' dissent, Senate-Democrat foreign-influence concerns over Oracle/Ellison) — and can a state challenge actually block or materially delay a deal the federal DOJ has already cleared without conditions? _(antitrust_regulatory)_
    - _why it matters:_ The principal residual close risk after DOJ clearance; sizes the gap between 'cleared' and 'closed' and the timeline to Q3 2026.

### Why this deal, why now
- Why this buyer, why now: what can David Ellison's Paramount Skydance — formed only in August 2025 and backed by Ellison-family / Oracle wealth and RedBird capital — do with WBD that Netflix or Comcast could not, and why launch a $100B+ megadeal within months of its own formation? _(deal_rationale_synergies)_
    - _why it matters:_ The synthesis 'why': distinguishes a specific strategic logic (scale to challenge Netflix/Disney; consolidate the streaming endgame) from empire-building or a politically-enabled land grab.
